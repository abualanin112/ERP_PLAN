# Business Data Glossary

## Purpose

This glossary defines business terms so UX, backend, database, and operations use the same language.

## Customer

A person or organization that may inquire, reserve, pay, sign documents, or use a property/unit.

## Lead

A potential sales opportunity before reservation confirmation.

## Lead Source

Where the lead came from, such as Facebook, referral, website, phone call, broker, or walk-in.

## Sales Pipeline

The staged journey used to move a lead from initial contact to booking, lost, or closed.

## Property

A real estate asset managed or marketed by the business.

## Building

A physical building that contains one or more units. It has a location, image, and general specifications.

## Unit

A rentable/sellable part of a property. Some properties may have one unit; others may have many.

## Company-Owned Unit

A unit owned by the company/office rather than an external owner.

## Reservation / Booking

A confirmed or draft allocation of a property/unit for a customer within a date range.

## Rental / Lease

The active business state after a reservation is activated or the customer arrives and the contract/rental is in effect.

## Contract

The legal/business record linked to a reservation, including customer, unit, dates, price, deposit, remaining balance, and accepted terms.

## Deposit

The amount paid before confirmation. In this business, it should be at least one day price unless an authorized override exists.

## Remaining Balance

The unpaid amount due at handover/check-in or according to configured policy.

## Availability

The business truth of whether a property/unit can be reserved for a date range.

## Check-In

The operational step that starts the customer stay/usage and records starting evidence.

## Check-Out

The operational step that ends the customer stay/usage and records ending evidence.

## Document

Any uploaded file linked to a business entity, such as national ID, contract, invoice, utility bill, or property media.

## OCR Result

The raw and parsed output extracted from a document/image by an OCR provider.

## Meter Reading

A starting or ending utility balance/value captured during check-in/check-out.

## Payment

A money movement or payment record linked to a customer, reservation, invoice, or future financial document.

## Daily Accounting Sheet

A daily operational finance summary containing contracts, units, dates, income, expenses, commissions, and collected money for accountant review.

## Owner

The person or organization that owns a property/unit.

## Commission

A business amount related to a contract or transaction. It may exist without always being registered as an expense on every contract.

## Agent

A user responsible for sales, follow-up, visits, or operational execution.

## Branch

A business subdivision that may own users, properties, leads, reservations, and reports.

## Worker

A person responsible for operational tasks such as cleaning, check-in/check-out support, or maintenance coordination.

## Maintenance Task

A service task for fixing or maintaining a unit/building. It can be external and may include vendor/cost details.

## Cleaning Task

An internal operational task usually performed by company workers to prepare or clean a unit.

## Furniture / Asset

Company-owned furniture or equipment assigned to a unit/apartment/hut and tracked for operational control.

## Annual Rent

A yearly rent obligation or due payment that needs date tracking and reminders.

## Paymob

The planned payment gateway connected to the manager's account, supporting channels such as Vodafone Cash, InstaPay, Fawry, and bank account.

## Price Movement

An indicator showing whether a unit price increased, decreased, or stayed the same compared with a previous price.

## Status

The explicit business state of a record, such as lead status, reservation status, document status, or payment status.

## Audit Trail

The historical record of important actions, actors, timestamps, and reasons.

## Read Model

A precomputed or optimized read structure used for reports or dashboards.

## Future Cache

A future performance layer used only when metrics prove the need. It is not the source of truth.
